ABB Robotics

ABB Robotics, a division of ABB Group, is a global leader in industrial automation and robotics. Founded in 1988 through the merger of ASEA and Brown Boveri, ABB has become a major innovator in robotic solutions for manufacturing, logistics, and heavy industry. ABB specializes in industrial robots, collaborative robots, robot software, and digital services.

The company’s robotic arms are widely used for welding, assembly, material handling, painting, and packaging. ABB is known for its advanced control software, including RobotStudio, which allows offline programming and simulation of robotic workflows, drastically reducing production downtime. ABB’s collaborative robots, the YuMi series, integrate human-robot collaboration safely, enhancing productivity in electronics assembly and small parts manufacturing. With operations in over 100 countries, ABB Robotics continues to drive innovation in smart factories and Industry 4.0 initiatives.
Headquarters: Zurich, Switzerland
Founded: 1988 (via merger)
Specializes in: Industrial automation & robotics.
