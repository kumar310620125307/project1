What is Robotics

Robotics is an interdisciplinary field that combines science, engineering, and technology to design, build, operate, and use robots.

What is Robot

A robot is a programmable, intelligent machine designed to perform tasks automatically or semi-automatically with speed, accuracy, and consistency. Modern robots combine mechanical components, electronic systems, sensors, actuators, and advanced software to imitate, support, or extend human abilities. The primary purpose of a robot is to reduce human effort, improve efficiency, increase safety, and perform tasks that may be dangerous, repetitive, or impossible for humans. Over the years, robots have evolved from simple mechanical devices to highly advanced autonomous systems capable of learning, adapting, and making intelligent decisions.
Robots are built around a few major components that enable their functionality. First is the mechanical structure — this includes the frame, joints, links, and body. These parts define the robot’s form, size, and movement capabilities. Industrial robots often use arms with multiple joints for precision handling, while humanoid robots simulate the human body with legs, hands, and head. Robots require actuators such as motors, hydraulic cylinders, pneumatic systems, or linear actuators to produce movement. These actuators allow robots to bend, rotate, lift, push, or manipulate objects in their environment.
